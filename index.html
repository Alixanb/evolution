Evolution: A Biological Simulation

Evolution is an interactive digital ecosystem where artificial life forms compete for resources and survive through generations. This simulation models the fundamental principles of biological evolution in a simplified 2D environment.

Core Simulation Features

  • Day-based Simulation: Creatures hunt for food, eat, and rest in a rhythmic cycle.
  • Population Dynamics: Real-time reproduction and mortality based on food consumption (feedScore).
  • Species Diversity:
    • Seekers: Efficient foragers that locate food quickly.
    • Raiders: Opportunistic creatures that steal resources from others.
  • Spatial Optimization: Utilizes a SpatialGrid for efficient O(1) food lookup, allowing hundreds of creatures simultaneously.
  • Real-time Analytics: Monitor population graphs, species distribution, and individual creature statistics.

How It Works

Every "day" in the simulation, a fixed amount of food is spawned across the grid. Creatures must find and consume at least one unit of food to survive. Consuming more food increases the chance of reproduction. The simulation handles complex interactions like food sharing and resource theft, leading to emergent behaviors and shifting population balances.
